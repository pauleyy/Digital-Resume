Every March 11, I celebrate my annual grow-a-year-older day. 19 years living, my current highest.

4 years of it was spent in high school at St. Bridget School Quezon City.

Another 3 years is being spent in college at De La Salle University. One more year till graduation!

Commuting is a harsh reality when you study in DLSU and live in Marikina. It takes about an hour or so just to arrive at school. Commuting grows on you, though. You'll learn to like it.

Interests

FOOD

Food is my first love. It started at and early age and still continue today. My body is an evidence of the love. I also like baking cupcakes. My sweet tooth usually craves for cupcakes and chocolates. That is the main reason I tried baking.

BOOKS

I love books. I have a special relationship with them. They make me experience emotions and adventures that would be impossible in real life. I usually read fiction but as long as it's good, I read. Not too much on school books, though.

MUSIC

Who doesn't love music? It's just too good to not love. There's something for everyone. Personally, I listen to bands. Not much into pop but there's the occasional 'I like that song' moment. I'm into the Wicked Soundtrack, currently. I have a Wicked hangover!

TECHNOLOGY

Technology and innovation, it's the reason why my degree exists. Learning about new technologies makes me believe that my childhood fantasies would come true. It makes me feel proud that I could be part of turning to reality.

Abilities

Does rolling your tongue and putting your fist in your mouth count?

I learned programming in C. Java came after I learned the basics from C. Then, database came along. MySQL became my friend. Now, HTML and CSS make my websites pretty. They are such a cute pair. Also, Python recently came along. I'm actually loving it. Loving them all, actually.

I'm an Apple user for 3 years. My baby is a Macbook Pro 2010. Being an Apple user, I'm quite versed in using Mac OS X. I started at Snow Leopard up to the current one, Mavericks. I still use Windows, though. I use it to run strictly Windows software. I have a Windows 8.1 virtual machine in my laptop. Yay for VMs! In handheld device, I use an Android phone, HTC One X. I do have an iPad that I could use whenever I like. Best of both worlds.

To help in the tasks I do, I have softwares that would make my a little more hassle-free. I have Microsoft Office and Apple iWork. I use both. It depends on the task, though, which one of the two choose. For Java programming, I use Eclipse. It's lightweight and easy to use. Partner it with Git and you have a collaborative project. I use Sublime Text for other programming languages.
